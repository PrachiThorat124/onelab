   • Secondary matching: (amount, date) fallback
   • Rounding tolerance: ±$0.01

🔑 KEY ASSUMPTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. PRIMARY MATCH: transaction_id (exact match)
2. FALLBACK MATCH: (amount, date) pair
3. ROUNDING TOLERANCE: ±$0.01 (standard for payments)
4. TIME WINDOW: 0–5 calendar days (accommodates bank delays)
5. DUPLICATES: Reported but not auto-corrected (requires human review)
6. FAILED TRANSACTIONS: Excluded (never settle anyway)
7. REFUNDS: Identified by negative amounts
8. CURRENCY: Single currency assumed (USD)

⚠️ PRODUCTION LIMITATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This solution is production-ready for:
  ✅ Small-to-medium datasets (<100K rows)
  ✅ Quick validation and testing
  ✅ Single-currency reconciliation
  ✅ Business-day based settlement windows

Consider these limitations:
  ⚠️ Scaling: O(n*m) algorithm; migrate to SQL for 1M+ rows
  ⚠️ Data quality: Assumes valid transaction_ids
  ⚠️ Time zones: May cause 1-day offset differences
  ⚠️ Concurrent duplicates: Can't distinguish bank vs merchant retry
  ⚠️ Secondary matching: (amount, date) must be unique per transaction

See SOLUTION.md Section 7 for detailed risk analysis and mitigations.
